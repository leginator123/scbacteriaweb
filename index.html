Understanding Salmonella

What is Salmonella?

Scientific Name: Salmonella enterica

Type: Bacteria

Salmonella is a group of bacteria that causes one of the most common forms of foodborne illness worldwide. Named after veterinarian Daniel E. Salmon, these bacteria have been making people sick since 1885.

How It Enters the Body

  • Contaminated food (raw meat, poultry, eggs)
  • Contaminated water sources
  • Contact with infected animals or surfaces
  • Pet food and animal habitats

Infection Process

1

Entry Point

Bacteria enters through the digestive tract via contaminated food or water. Incubation: 6 hours to 6 days.

2

Invasion

Salmonella survives stomach acid and invades intestinal cells, destroying the lining that absorbs water.

3

Spread

In severe cases, bacteria enters bloodstream (bacteremia), potentially affecting tissues throughout the body.

4

Symptoms

Diarrhea, fever, stomach cramps, nausea, vomiting. Typically lasts 4-7 days.

High-Risk Groups

Infants & Children Under 5

Developing immune systems are more vulnerable to severe infection

Adults Over 65

Weakened immune defenses increase complication risk

Immunocompromised

HIV/AIDS, chemotherapy, organ transplant patients face higher danger

Chronic Conditions

IBD, sickle cell disease, low stomach acid from medications

How Your Body Fights Salmonella

First Line Defense (Innate Immunity)

Physical Barriers

  • Stomach Acid: Kills many bacteria before reaching intestines
  • Intestinal Lining: Forms protective barrier against invasion
  • Good Bacteria: Compete with Salmonella for resources

Cellular Response

  • Macrophages: Engulf and destroy bacteria (phagocytosis)
  • Inflammation: Increases blood flow, brings immune cells to infection site
  • Fever: Raises body temperature to inhibit bacterial growth

Adaptive Immunity (Learned Defense)

Key Players

  • B-Cells: Produce antibodies that tag bacteria for destruction
  • Antibodies (IgG, IgM): Neutralize bacteria and mark them for removal
  • T-Cells: Coordinate immune response and kill infected cells
  • Memory Cells: Remember Salmonella for faster future response

Immune System Tissues Involved

  • Lymph Nodes: Filter and trap pathogens
  • Spleen: Filters blood, removes damaged cells
  • Bone Marrow: Produces new immune cells
  • Gut-Associated Lymphoid Tissue: Monitors intestinal threats

How Salmonella Evades Your Defenses

  • Camouflage: Hides inside cells where antibodies can't reach
  • Speed: Multiplies rapidly before full immune response activates
  • Immune Suppression: Some strains interfere with macrophage function
  • Mutation: Over 2,500 serotypes make broad immunity challenging

SalmoShield™: Our Enhancement

What is SalmoShield™?

SalmoShield™ is an advanced vaccine that trains your adaptive immune system to recognize and rapidly respond to multiple Salmonella serotypes before infection occurs.

How It Works

1. Safe Antigen Delivery

Introduces weakened or inactive Salmonella antigens (surface proteins) via intramuscular injection

If real Salmonella enters body, memory cells trigger immediate antibody production—neutralizing bacteria before illness develops
